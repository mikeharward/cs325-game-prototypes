Evade the Guards!

  • Originality: What is original about your game?
    This is a top down stealth game, where you try to avoid guards to reach checkpoints. The concept has been done many times before, but the originality in a game like this comes from the unique paths you can make from the enemies' movement.
  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?
    Making the patrol paths was interesting, I used a global timer and moved them in a direction for half of it, then switched directions for the other half. This is why all the enemies turn around at the same time. If I wanted to make a more complex path or have out of sync enemies I could add additional timers or split the timer into more segments than just 2.
    Making the player movement feel natural was also interesting, I added quite a few checks to have the movement act in the manner I wanted so it doesn't feel clunky, which is important in a movement-based game like this.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype? What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?
    I hadn't done movement in this manner before, and I was really pleased with the way it turned out. I didn't know if a timer was a good way to approach the patrol paths, but it ended up working out quite well.

  • Prototype Goal: What game mechanic is this prototype evaluating?
    The core mechanic for this prototype is the interaction between the player and the enemies, primarily the feel of the movement and hitboxes.
  • Player Experience Goals: What experience do you want players to have when playing your game?
    I want players to feel pressured to escape, and to try and think ahead to plan a route through the guards. The game should provide a decent balance between skill and strategy.
